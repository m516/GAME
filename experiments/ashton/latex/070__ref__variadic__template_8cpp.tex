\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
\begin{DoxyAttention}{Attention}
At the time of writing, the part between $<$$>$ is totally ignored\+: func$<$Args...$>$(Args... args) is interpreted as func(Args... args).

Beware that a function parameter with either a \& or $\ast$ operator, e.\+g. \textquotesingle{}const Args\&... args\textquotesingle{}, requires \textbackslash{}link and \textbackslash{}ref to specify such parameter as verbatim, i.\+e. \textquotesingle{}const Args\&... args\textquotesingle{}. At the time of writing, the form func(const Args\&...) will fail, unless the function parameter was declared just as \textquotesingle{}const Args\&...\textquotesingle{}. 
\end{DoxyAttention}
